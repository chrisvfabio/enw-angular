Nordic Walking

Taking walking to the next level, it's a new way of walking.

Nordic Walking is relatively new to Australia but practised widely in Europe, the U.K. and the U.S.A. Its origins are in cross-country skiing, as the name suggests. It is a fitness activity utilising specially designed poles and a technique which activates 90% of the body’s muscles. It can be done on a variety of surfaces including footpaths, grass, sand and bush tracks.

It is suitable for people of all ages and fitness levels as well as those living with chronic medical conditions. Before commencing any exercise program, it is advisable to get the go-ahead from a medical professional.

As an instructor and regular Nordic Walker, I promote Nordic Walking as a way of keeping the brain healthy and reducing the risk of dementia. Learning Nordic Walking is simultaneously mentally, physically and socially stimulating. Activities that combine these 3 elements have been shown to be particularly beneficial for brain health.

To obtain the benefits of Nordic Walking listed below, it is important to learn the correct technique from a qualified instructor.

Benefits

Scientific research supports the following benefits from Nordic Walking using the correct technique:

  • Up to 40% more calories burned compared to regular walking
  • Up to 25% cardiovascular benefit than regular walking
  • Improves co-ordination and balance
  • Improves walking posture
  • Releases pain and tension in the neck and shoulders

To view the scientific literature, go to: www.nordicacademy.com.au
